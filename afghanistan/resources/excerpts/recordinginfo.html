RECORDING INFO

Several reissue albums of recordings from the period of my time in Afghanistan have appeared, including my own repackaging of my field tapes, based on lp albums that appeared in 1969-1972, which appeared in 2003 on Traditional Crossroads in a 2-cd set entitled Afghanistan in Peaceful Times.

Some of the genres and performers represented here appear on records of the 1960s and early 1970s:

The Music of Afghanistan (Alain Danielou) #5 in the influential UNESCO series "A Musical Anthology of the Orient," ca. 1965.

Afghanistan et Iran (J.-C. and S. Lubtchansky), Collection Musee de l'Homme, Vogue LVLX-191, 1969.

Folk Music of Afghanistan (Hiromi Lorraine Sakata), 2 lp discs, Lyrichord LLST 7230-31

Musiques Classiques & Populaires d'Afghanistan (Bernard Dupaigne), Anthologie de la Musique des Peuples, SFPP-AMP 72901, 1973 recordings.

Musique de la Zone Interdite du Nouristan (Yves Somavilla), Barclay XBLY 920 086, 1968 recordings.

Albums issued in cd format include:

Deben Bhattacharya's early recordings (1955, 1970) have been re-issued on cd as Inside Afghanistan, ARC Music FUCD 1722, 2002.

Other releases include: Afghanistan: The Rubab of Herat (John Baily), VDE CD-699, 1993.

Afghanistan: Female Musicians of Herat (Veronica Doubleday and John Baily), Unesco Collection, Audivis, 2002.

Ustad Mohamad Omar:Virtuoso from Afghanistan (Lorraine Sakata), Smithsonian Folkways SWF CD 40439, 2002, based on 1974 tape.

Afghanistan: A Journey to an Unknown Musical World (Westdeutsche Rundfunk), World Network LC 6759, has vintage material similar to the present album.

Shirin Dahani:Music of North Afghanistan (Jan van Belle), Pan 2089, 2001, was taped in 1996 before the Taliban occupation of the North.

There are many fine recent albums covering music of the peoples just across the border in Central Asia. A helpful compendium honoring the visit of many troupes to the US in 2002 is: The Silk Road: A Musical Caravan, Smithsonian Folkways SWF CD40438.
